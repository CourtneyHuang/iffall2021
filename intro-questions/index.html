6. https://www.printmag.com I think the design of the Print Magazine website is very effective through its use of visual hierarchy to help the viewer navigate the page. Each landing page highlights featured articles at the top and are a bigger size and all other articles are listed below in grid format. The menu at the top of the website also divides it up by section/topic to better help find what you are looking for. These aspects use design to make the user experience easier.

7. https://www.studiomoross.com/ This is the website for the graphic design firm Studio Moross. I think it effectively communicates it purpose because the opening page immediately displays their work and even autoplays clips of animated or video projects instead of just a thumbnail from them. The side bar on the website also allows you to narrow your search of projects and makes it easier for clients to find out if Studio Moross can produce the kind of work they're looking for.

8. https://opensource.glassanimals.com/ The website for the band Glass Animals works well because it is very reflective of the band and genre of music they produce. The site mimicks an old desktop browser. The music glass animals makes is kind of techno and alternative and a lot of these album cover art holds similar influences to the design of their website.
